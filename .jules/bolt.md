## 2024-05-24 - Stateful filtering optimization in Monte Carlo loop\n**Learning:** In the Pocock-Simon minimization engine, eagerly filtering the active pool of available strata combinations on every iteration of a large Monte Carlo simulation causes significant performance overhead ((N)$ filter operations inside the loop). Because pool items are only invalidated when a stratum cap is reached, filtering is actually unnecessary most of the time.\n**Action:** Introduce a `poolNeedsFilter` boolean flag, initialized to `true`, and only execute the pool filtering logic when this flag is active. Reset the flag to `false` after filtering, and selectively switch it back to `true` only inside the state-update logic when a counter equals or exceeds its defined cap. This short-circuits thousands of redundant array iterations in hot paths.
